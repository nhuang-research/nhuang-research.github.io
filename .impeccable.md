## Design Context

### Users
This site is primarily for academic peers, prospective collaborators, students, institutional stakeholders, and media visitors who want to quickly understand Nina Huang's research expertise, editorial leadership, and professional standing. On the About page, their main job is to get a fast, credible overview and know how to connect or learn more.

### Brand Personality
Scholarly, warm, modern. The site should feel credible and accomplished without becoming cold or overly corporate. It should project intellectual clarity, generosity, and contemporary confidence.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a refined academic editorial style with restrained warmth and clear visual hierarchy. Build from existing University of Miami orange and green accents, but apply them strategically through tinted neutrals, subtle gradients, and purposeful emphasis rather than loud saturation. Avoid generic tech gradients, pure grayscale, or flashy startup styling.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with credibility: make expertise, appointments, and research standing easy to scan.
2. Use color with intent: let Miami orange and green guide emphasis, links, and section rhythm rather than overwhelming the page.
3. Keep it human: balance institutional authority with warmth, openness, and approachability.
4. Prefer editorial clarity over decorative clutter: strong typography, breathable spacing, and a few memorable accents beat many competing visual effects.
